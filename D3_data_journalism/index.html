Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

There is a strong positive relationship between in Poverty (%) and Lacks Healthcare (%). Values in Poverty (%) range from 9% to 22%. Conversly, Lacks Healthcare (%) vs. Household Income (Median) shows a slight negative relationship. As expected, the higher the median household income, the lower the percent lacking healthcare.

There is a strong positive relationship between percent of smokers and in Poverty (%). Conversly, median incomes between 40,000 and 55,000 the data shows a strong negative relationship between percent smokers and median household income. However, once a median household income of 55,000 is reached, there appears to be a leaveling off of the percent smokers. In this range, the percent smokers stabilizes between 13%-22% (with 1 outlier at 10%)

There is a weak positive relationship between percent obese and in Poverty (%). Conversly, there is a weak negative relationship between percent obese and median household income.

Plots against median age do not show a strong or weak relationship with percent obese, percent smokers, or percent lacking healthcare. Median ages range between 30 and 44.
